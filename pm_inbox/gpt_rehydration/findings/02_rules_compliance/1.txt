## Section 2: Design Decisions Needed

### DD-01 — ID schema: human-readable vs opaque, and where minting lives

* **Tension:**

  * *Human-readable IDs* aid debugging and manual authoring, but risk drift, collisions, and normalization edge-cases.
  * *Opaque IDs* (monotonic ints / hash-based) are safer for determinism but harder to debug.
* **Why this matters now:** BL-017 bans auto UUID generation, implying you need a deterministic, reproducible ID minting path.
* **Decision output:** Single-page spec: formats, minting authority, persistence boundary, collision policy, and “rename/deletion” semantics.

### DD-02 — Voice descriptor vocabulary and resolution strategy

* **Tension:**

  * Free-form descriptors are expressive but hard to map deterministically.
  * Controlled vocab is rigid but testable and stable.
* **Constraint:** Resolver must be pure, session-scoped cache only, and reference audio selection must be string matching (no embeddings).

### DD-03 — “Structured emission” canonical artifact naming: Scene Fact Pack vs NarrativeBrief vs Truth Packet

* **Tension:** Prior docs refer to “Scene Fact Pack,” newer state shows **NarrativeBrief** in the pipeline. Without a synthesis, teams will implement incompatible contracts.
