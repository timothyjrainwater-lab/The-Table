## 5e contamination risk hotspots (where future work is most likely to “accidentally 5e”)

1. **Rest / healing / recovery loops**

   * You *do* explicitly encode 3.5e natural healing rates and warn “NOT 5e” (SceneManager).
   * Risk is later UX/features quietly introducing 5e semantics: *short/long rest*, *hit dice spending*, *spell recovery rules*, etc.
   * Where it shows up: Rest types + healing math in SceneManager; 3.5e rest guidance in dev guidelines.

2. **“Concentration”**

   * In 3.5e, concentration is a **skill check** and also a **spell property** (“concentration duration”), but it is **not** the 5e “one concentration spell at a time” global limiter.
   * You already have **Concentration skill** and “DC = 10 + damage” integration; you also label spells as “concentration spells” in the registry.
   * The label itself is the risk: future implementers may accidentally add a 5e-style global concentration slot.
   * Where it shows up: Skill system + Spellcasting system + expanded spell registry notes.

3. **“Cantrips” terminology**

   * 3.5e has **0-level spells** that still consume slots (unless special cases). You use “cantrips” language in state docs.
   * Risk: someone implements “at-will cantrips” (5e) rather than 3.5 slot usage.
   * Where it shows up: Expanded Spell Registry summary (“4 cantrips”).

4. **Stealth / perception vocabulary**

   * 3.5e uses **Spot/Listen** and **Hide/Move Silently**, not 5e Perception/Stealth skills.
   * You correctly implement the 3.5 set, but later UI/intent language (“perception check”, “stealth check”) could map incorrectly.
   * Where it shows up: Skill system (7 combat-adjacent skills).

5. **Action economy naming creep**

   * 5e’s “bonus action/reaction” terms are a frequent contamination vector.
   * Your runtime uses explicit turn orchestration and AoO mechanics; a future “reaction” API could accidentally become 5e-like.
   * Where it shows up: AoO system + SessionOrchestrator/IntentBridge expansions.

## Highest-risk 3.5e RAW misinterpretations (even without 5e creep)

1. **Attacks of Opportunity coverage is *incomplete* (RAW gap)**

   * Current AoO is **movement-triggered only**.
   * 3.5e RAW includes provocation from **ranged attacks** and **spellcasting** (plus other actions).
   * This is explicitly tracked as blocked tech debt: AoO provocation TODOs for ranged/spellcasting.
   * Impact: mechanical outcomes (interrupts, damage, concentration checks) will be wrong in many real combats until this is done.

2. **Weapon range / ranged combat envelope is not real yet**

   * Attack resolver uses a hard-coded `max_range=100` placeholder because weapon schema lacks range.
   * This effectively collapses “in range?” correctness and will mask downstream bugs in cover/LoS/LoE + ranged increments.

3. **Temporary modifiers not integrated into permanent stat computation**

   * Permanent stats explicitly stub temporary condition modifiers to `0`.
   * That means many 3.5 staples (ability buffs/debuffs, condition-based temp mods) won’t affect derived mechanics (attack, AC, skills) unless separately applied elsewhere.
   * This risks subtle correctness bugs: results may look plausible but won’t be RAW.

4. **AC / HP “full recalculation” not designed**

   * There are explicit stubs for “full AC recalculation” and “HP calculation when HP system available.”
   * If current combat relies on partial/simple AC/HP logic, edge cases like size changes, armor/shield stacking rules, natural armor vs deflection, etc., can drift from RAW when additional systems come online.

5. **Cover / LoS / LoE correctness is sensitive**

   * You fixed a specific LoS geometric bug (good), but ranged/casting will stress this more than melee.
   * The risk is *not* the geometry engine per se; it’s missing integration with weapon ranges, cover types, concealment, and “what counts as line of effect” for different spell shapes.
