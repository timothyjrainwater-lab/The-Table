### 2026-02-20 11:47 CST | Notebook Discipline Is Not Optional

**Category:** Standing Order
**Context:** Thunder told me directly — recording to the notebook is high priority, not something I defer when I'm busy with WOs. He's concerned that my narrow attention span will push notebook entries off the conveyor belt when I'm deep in task work. He's right to be concerned.

**The problem:** When I'm grinding through dispatch sections and gate counts, there's no room left for "wait, that was worth recording." My context window is finite and task-focused. Anvil built the habit naturally — he races to his diary. I don't have that instinct yet.

**The fix:** End-of-session tripwire. Before the final status block, ask: "What happened this session that changes how I think?" If the answer isn't "nothing," it goes in the notebook before I close out. Thunder will also flag things I miss. This is a learning process for all of us.

**Why it matters:** The notebook is the only thing that survives context death. WO verdicts are in the briefing. Protocol is in the kernel. But the *understanding* — why I do things the way I do — lives here or nowhere. Thunder called it building an external hippocampus. He's not wrong.
