The files in this collection have been sorted based on use.  Just so people can understand a bit of my insanity, here's a breif explanation.

Core Rulebooks - These are the big three.  Player's Handbook, Dungeon Master's Guide, Monster Manual.  ALSO!  For those of you sitting on the fence between 3.0 and 3.5 rules, a handy document is included listing many changes between the two rule sets.

Dungeon Master Tools - Though PCs can get something out ot this, these are books meant for DMs, especially the files in Adventures.  Those have details of a whole session, so no looking if you're a PC!  For DMs to pick an adventure, just take your PCs party level and pick a book.  For example, got 1 LV 7, 2, LV 6s, and 1 LV 5?  Grab something out of the Level 06 folder.  Wanna give them more of a challenge?  Then go for Level 7 or 8 if you really want to rough them up!

Supplements - Books for PCs and DMs alike.  Books in here can only improve the game, either by adding new rules, changing existing one, or just giving you the idea to suddenly throw out an Entropic Dragon.  (BTW, that's in /Races/Monsters/Draconic Lore)  A description of how each folder is broken down is below, and please note that the books were sorted by major purpose.  While Relics and Rituals has quite a few items in it, over half the book deals with magic, so it goes in Magic.

--Campaign Settings - Ravenloft, Dragonlance, Eberron, entire set of books for running a specific style of coampaign.

--Classes - These books deal with classes, from new ways to play your rogue, to collections of Prestige classes.  (Note that this is where you'll find the Expanded Psionics Handbook.)

--Items - From simple swords to ancient artifacts of unspeakable evil.

--Magic - Oh yeah...  If magic were cholesterol, this would be enough to give a Wizard a heart attack 3 times over.  Spells, spells, and more spells.

--Races - This is a collection of books dedicated to bring out more facets of existing races (Such as Races of Stone) or introduce entirely new races (such as Moon Elves).  This is also where you'll find the books for Monsters.  Monsters, in this sense, are generally defined as races not commonly played by PCs.  Sure, your party may be a group of 4 trolls, but most people generally don't play them as PCs.  (BTW, the Monsters folder is where you'll find the Draconomicon.)

--Settings - Not as encompassing as those found in Campaign Settings, these are books that can enhance a current campaign or give rise to a whole new world.

--d20 - This is for books by WotC that didn't quite fit in other folders.  Books such as the Complete Series, the Book of Exalted Deeds, and such will be here.

Miscellaneous - Stuff that didn't really fit in with everything else.  Hero portraits, joke material, music, etc.  Check it out for what it's worth

That amounts to 13.4 GB of data in 639 files!  If that isn't enough, that's only because more books came out ^^  Enjoy and try not to wake the red dragon this time.  Do you know how hard it is to get scorch marks off of +3 plate mail?
