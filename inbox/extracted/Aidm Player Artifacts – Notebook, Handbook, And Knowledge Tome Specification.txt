PurposeThis document captures and locks the player-owned artifacts that complete the AIDM table experience:the personal notebookthe player handbook (rules tome)the evolving knowledge/experience tomeThese artifacts restore the player side of the table and are foundational to immersion, learning, trust, and emotional engagement.Core PrincipleThe player must own something.Not everything belongs to the DM or the system.Dice, memory, and narration are shared — but notes and understanding belong to the player.1) Player Notebook (Freeform, Player-Owned)DefinitionA personal, infinite notebook that belongs exclusively to the player.It is: - freeform - non-authoritative - non-gamified - persistentCapabilitiesThe notebook must support: - freehand drawing - handwritten-style text input - simple shapes - doodling - loose diagrams (maps, symbols, reminders)No scoring, validation, or correction is applied.UI PhilosophyMinimal, tactile, tool-like (pen, brush, eraser)Simple radial or contextual tool selectionMouse / stylus behaves like a penNo complex menusThe notebook should feel physical, not technical.DM Interaction with NotebookThe player may ask: - “DM, can you make a note of that for me?” - “Put that in my notebook for later.”The DM may: - add brief notes - organize entries into loose sections (e.g., “Things Learned”)The DM never edits or corrects player-written notes unless explicitly asked.Design ConstraintsThe notebook may contain incorrect or incomplete information.This is intentional and must not be corrected automatically.It represents the player’s memory, not objective truth.2) Player Handbook / Rules TomeDefinitionA searchable, indexed reference that fulfills the role of the physical Player’s Handbook.It is not meant to be read linearly.Content ScopeSystem concepts (stats, actions, conditions)Generalized rules explanations (rephrased, non-copyrighted)Indexed entries and glossary-style accessExact rule text is avoided; intent and function are emphasized.InteractionSearchable by concept or termClickable references from narration or ledgerDM may direct players to entries conversationallyExample: > “If you want more detail, check your handbook under ‘Grappling.’”3) Knowledge / Experience Tome (Discoverable)DefinitionA living record of what the player character knows, not what the system knows.This tome grows through play.ContentsMay include: - discovered monsters (descriptive, not full stat blocks) - learned spell effects (only if identified) - known locations - factions and NPCs encountered - XP milestonesKnowledge GatingInformation appears only when: - the character has perceived it - the character has learned it through action or experienceUnknown information does not appear at all.Progressive DetailEntries may evolve: - “Goblin — small, aggressive” - later: “Fast, lightly armored” - later still: “Defense appears around 15”Exact values only appear if explicitly learned.Relationship Between ArtifactsNotebook = player memory (subjective, messy)Handbook = system knowledge (objective, reference)Knowledge Tome = character knowledge (earned, partial)They must remain distinct.Accessibility ConsiderationsAll artifacts must support text-only interactionScreen-reader compatibility requiredFont size and contrast adjustableWhy This MattersThese artifacts: - complete the tabletop ritual - support learning without pressure - preserve mystery - enable reflection - build emotional ownershipThey are not optional UX elements.StatusThis document locks player-owned artifacts as core experiential systems, not auxiliary features.
