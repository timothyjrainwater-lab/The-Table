PurposeThis document captures and locks a critical transparency and teaching system that complements the voice-first philosophy without undermining it.It defines a minimal but authoritative mechanics output window that: - proves fairness and determinism - supports accessibility - teaches new players implicitly - builds trust through visible recordsThis is not a control surface. It is a ledger and explanation aid.Core PrincipleTrust requires visibility.Even in a voice-first, narrative-driven system, players must be able to: - see numbers - see rolls - see bonuses - see outcomesThis visibility is optional, non-intrusive, and always truthful.Mechanics Output Window (“The Ledger”)DefinitionA lightweight, scrollable output panel that displays mechanical facts in real time.Examples: - dice results - modifiers and bonuses - stat values - success/failure outcomesThis window exists alongside voice narration.Design ConstraintsThe ledger does not require interaction to function.It does not compete visually with the DM.It does not present configuration or controls.It is readable at a glance and skimmable.Placement should be peripheral (side or corner).Accessibility RoleVisual AccessibilitySupports players who cannot rely on audio alone.Font size and contrast must be adjustable.Information must not rely on color alone.Cognitive AccessibilityClear numeric breakdowns reduce mental load.Players can review outcomes at their own pace.Trust & Fairness AuthenticationDice TransparencyFor every roll, the ledger should show: - dice type (e.g., d20) - raw roll - applied modifiers - final resultThis acts as proof of fairness.Deterministic ProofThe ledger represents: - what actually happened - what was logged - what can be replayedIt is a visible reflection of the deterministic engine.Teaching & Learning FunctionPassive EducationNew players learn by seeing: - how stats influence rolls - where bonuses come from - how difficulty affects outcomesNo tutorial text is required for learning to begin.Active Inquiry SupportPlayers may ask: - “Why is this number so high?” - “Where did this bonus come from?”The DM must be able to reference ledger entries and explain them conversationally.Relationship to Player ModelingFrequent ledger usage may signal analytical/system-focused players.Minimal usage may signal narrative-first preference.The DM may adapt explanation depth accordingly.BoundariesThe ledger never changes outcomes.The ledger never allows editing of values.The ledger never replaces DM narration.It is observational, not authoritative.Non-GoalsThis is not a character sheet editor.This is not a combat log spam feed.This is not a debugging console.StatusThis document locks the Mechanics Output Window (Teaching Ledger) as a core trust, accessibility, and education system.
