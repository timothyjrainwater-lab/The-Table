PurposeThis document captures and locks a previously implicit but critical design pillar: continuous player modeling and adaptive DM behavior.It formalizes how the system: - categorizes players - updates understanding over time - uses those signals to adapt tone, pacing, explanation depth, and presentationThis is not personalization as cosmetics; it is interaction competence.Core Insight (Re-highlighted)Player categorization is not an onboarding artifact.It is a persistent, evolving model that informs every DM response across the lifetime of a campaign.The DM is not just responding to actions; it is: - observing - tagging - inferring - adjustingPlayer Model (High-Level)The system maintains a Player Profile that is continuously updated.This profile is: - explicit (direct answers) - implicit (behavioral inference) - revisable (never locked permanently)Core Player Dimensions (Initial Set)The following dimensions must be tracked at minimum:1. Experience LevelNew to TTRPGsExperienced playerSystem-mastery-orientedSignals: - requests for explanations - skip behavior - language used when describing intent2. Desired PacingSlow / ceremonialBalancedFast / outcome-focusedSignals: - skipping dice animations - impatience markers - explicit requests3. Explanation Depth PreferenceTeach me everythingExplain when neededNo explanationsSignals: - follow-up questions - acceptance of summaries4. Tonal PreferenceSeriousPlayfulDarkCozyDramaticSignals: - reactions to DM tone - language mirroring5. Communication ModalityVoice-firstText-firstMixedSignals: - dominant input channel - fallback usageTagging & Inference MechanismExplicit TaggingDuring onboarding and conversation, the DM may directly ask: - “Do you want explanations or should I just give results?” - “Do you like things slow and descriptive, or quick and punchy?”Answers directly update player profile tags.Implicit InferenceThe system must also infer preferences from behavior: - repeated skipping → prefers speed - repeated clarification requests → needs explanation - short commands → system-savvyInferred tags have confidence weights and may decay over time.Adaptive DM Output (Critical)The DM must adapt:1. Language ComplexityChild-friendly vs. system shorthand2. Narrative DensityRich description vs. concise summary3. Tonality & AffectFormal vs. casualWarm vs. neutralSerious vs. humorous4. Intervention FrequencyFrequent guidance vs. hands-offThis adaptation happens without re-asking every time.GuardrailsNon-Determinism BoundaryPlayer modeling affects presentation, not mechanics.Outcomes must remain unchanged.Transparency & ReversibilityPlayer can always say:“Explain more.”“Skip this.”“Talk to me like I know what I’m doing.”Explicit overrides trump inferred tags.Storage & IntegrationPlayer profile stored separately from campaign state.Referenced by DM during narration, explanation, and onboarding.Used to parameterize LLM prompts (tone, verbosity, teaching level).Why This MattersWithout persistent player modeling: - the DM feels inconsistent - explanations feel random - tone mismatches cause frictionWith it: - the DM feels attentive - learning feels natural - engagement deepens over timeStatusThis document elevates player categorization and adaptive DM behavior to a core system, not a UX flourish.
