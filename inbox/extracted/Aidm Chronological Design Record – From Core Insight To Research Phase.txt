PurposeThis document is a chronological design record capturing the evolution of AIDM’s architecture, constraints, and priorities as they emerged through discussion.Its role is historical and operational: - Preserve why decisions were made - Prevent regression or re-litigation - Serve as onboarding and alignment materialThis is not speculative. It records what has been established.Phase 1 — Foundational Insight: Mechanics vs PresentationInitial RealizationThe system is deterministic at its core. Mechanics, numbers, legality, and outcomes are invariant.From this, the key realization emerged:If mechanics are stable, fiction is interchangeable.This led to the explicit separation of: - Canonical mechanical core (IDs, rules, state) - Presentation layer (names, descriptions, visuals, audio, voice, language)This was identified as an architectural property, not a feature request.Phase 2 — Reskinning Is Consequence, Not Scope CreepClarificationGenre reskinning (fantasy → sci‑fi → cyberpunk) was examined.Conclusion: - Reskinning is feasible and correct only if treated as presentation - It becomes scope creep only when promised as shipped contentKey boundary established: - Possible ≠ RequiredThe system must not hard-code fantasy assumptions.Phase 3 — Generative Assets, Not Shipped AssetsShift in FramingIt was clarified that AIDM does not ship fixed assets.Instead, it ships: - Constraints - Guarantees - InterfacesAssets (images, sound, voice) are generated either: - During campaign/session preparation, or - On-demand under controlled circumstancesThis reframed concerns about “content explosion” into concerns about quality control.Phase 4 — Image Generation Risk IdentifiedKey RiskPoor image quality is immediately visible and breaks immersion.Critical insight: > Image generation without image quality evaluation is unacceptable.Resulting decisions: - Image generation must be paired with a critique / quality evaluation step - Generation primarily occurs during prep, not live playPhase 5 — Visual Presentation Model ChosenSprite / Portrait ModelInspired by classic JRPGs (e.g., FF6, FFT):NPCs receive static anchor portraits generated onceScenes use background plates generated per location/beatRuntime presentation is compositing, not constant generationBenefits: - Continuity - Reduced compute - Stable visual identityPhase 6 — Memory, Indexing, and ContinuityCore ObservationLLM coherence degrades when it is forced to “remember everything.”Decision: - Truth lives outside the LLM - LLM queries indexed records instead of holding stateMemory is defined as structured records: - Canonical entity cards - Event timelines - Relationships - Open threads - Inventory and stateThis indexing layer is essential to runtime stability.Phase 7 — Voice as Primary InterfacePriority ShiftVoice output quality was identified as more immersion-critical than voice input accuracy.Key points: - Poor TTS breaks trust immediately - AIDM must control tone, pacing, and expression - STT errors are recoverable; TTS quality is notDesign implication: - TTS quality and control are high-priority research targets - STT must include disambiguation and confirmation for high-impact actionsPhase 8 — Sound Generation as Prep-Time Asset PipelineScope DefinitionSound (SFX, ambience, music) is treated as: - Pre-generated palettes during prep - Tagged and archived for reuseRuntime behavior: - Selection and mixing, not constant generationThis improves immersion while preserving determinism and performance.Phase 9 — Multilingual Support ClarifiedOutput LocalizationAll player-facing text rendered via templatesLanguage packs are importableInput Localization (Explicit Requirement)Players may speak or type in multiple languagesAlias tables map localized input to canonical IDsThe engine never consumes localized strings.Phase 10 — Hardware Reality CheckConstraint IdentifiedThe system must run on median consumer hardware.The Steam Hardware Survey was identified as the reference baseline.Implications: - Model size and latency matter - GPU cannot be assumed - CPU fallback paths requiredPhase 11 — Formal Research Phase InitiatedResearch Tracks DefinedLLM (constraint adherence, coherence, multilingual)Image generation + image critiqueSTT (multilingual, accuracy)TTS (naturalness, control)Hardware and optimization (cross-cutting)Gating RuleNo model may be integrated without: - Known failure modes - Acceptable resource footprint - Compatibility with determinism and presentation isolationCurrent StateArchitectural separation is lockedGenerative assets are prep-first, quality-gatedVoice and coherence are top experiential prioritiesResearch phase is mandatory before implementation lock-inThis document represents the current authoritative understanding of AIDM’s design trajectory.
