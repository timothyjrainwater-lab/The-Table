**Official Rules**:
- Middle Age: -1 Str/Dex/Con, +1 Int/Wis/Cha
- Old Age: -2 Str/Dex/Con, +1 Int/Wis/Cha (cumulative)

## 21. INSTANTANEOUS DURATION VS PERMANENT - DISPELLABILITY

**[CAP-GATE: Dispelling]** **[SKR-DEP: Duration Classification]** **[C-08: Duration Clarity]**

### A. Original Community Question
"Wall of Stone has instantaneous duration. Does that mean you can't dispel it, but you CAN dispel a Wall of Force which is permanent?"

### B. Core Uncertainty
Whether "instantaneous" duration spells create effects that persist independently of magic (and thus can't be dispelled) vs "permanent" duration spells that maintain active magic (and thus can be dispelled).

### C. Formalized Problem Statement
How does spell duration terminology (instantaneous vs permanent vs (permanent) with parentheses) determine whether an effect can be dispelled?

### D. Source Context
- Giant in the Playground: "Permanent vs Until Dispelled" [Archive]
- EN World: "Permanent Spells"
- D&D 3.5 SRD: Duration (Spell Descriptor)

**Official RAW**:
- **Instantaneous**: "The effect of a spell with an instantaneous duration can't be dispelled, because the magical effect is already over before the dispel magic can take effect"
- **Permanent**: Effect remains and is vulnerable to dispel magic
- Wall of Stone special case: "If you maintain your concentration on this spell for its whole duration, the wall becomes permanent and can't be dispelled"

**Community Debate**:
- Why does Wall of Stone need to explicitly say "can't be dispelled" if instantaneous spells already can't be?
- Implication: Maybe permanent effects normally CAN be dispelled unless stated otherwise

**[CONSENSUS-WEAK]**: Different tables rule differently on whether "permanent" = "always dispellable"

## 23. ANTIMAGIC FIELD - SUPPRESSION VS DISPELLING

**[CAP-GATE: Suppression]** **[SKR-DEP: Magic Negation]** **[C-09: Removal Requirements]**

### A. Original Community Question
"My Permanency'd Darkvision got hit by an Antimagic Field. Is it gone forever or does it come back?"

### B. Core Uncertainty
Whether Antimagic Field permanently destroys magical effects or temporarily suppresses them, and what happens to ongoing spell durations.

### C. Formalized Problem Statement
How does antimagic field interact with permanent spells, magic items, and ongoing spell durations?

### D. Source Context
- D&D 3.5 SRD: Antimagic Field
- EN World: "Casting spells in Antimagic Fields"
- D&D Beyond: "How does Antimagic Field Affect certain spells"

**Official Mechanics**:
- **Suppresses, does NOT dispel**: Magic items/spells stop functioning but aren't destroyed
- **Magic items**: Temporarily become non-magical, properties return when leaving field
- **Permanent spells**: Suppressed in field, resume when field ends or they leave
- **Duration counting**: Time spent suppressed DOES count against spell duration

**Specific Cases**:
- Summoned creatures: Wink out, reappear when field ends, duration continues counting
- Spell resistance applies: Must beat SR to suppress a summoned creature
- Golems/constructs: Unaffected (magic is part of their creation, not sustained)
- Artifacts: Unaffected by mortal magic

**[STOP]**: Antimagic Field exceptions (Wall of Force, Prismatic Sphere/Wall) have "remain unaffected" clause

### D. Source Context
- EN World: "Dispel Magic & Permanency?"
- Paizo Forums: "Dispel Magic and Permanency"
- Giant in the Playground: "Spells to Permanency [3.5]" [Archive]

**Official Rules**:
- **On yourself**: "This application of permanency can be dispelled only by a caster of higher level than you were when you cast the spell"

**Official RAW**:
- "The ready action lets you prepare to take an action later, after your turn is over but before your next one has begun"
- "The action occurs just before the action that triggers it"
- "If the triggered action is part of another character's activities, **you interrupt** the other character"

1. **Interruption**: Readied actions occur "just before" the trigger, interrupting the triggering action
2. **Permanent initiative change**: Once you act on a readied action, that becomes your new initiative permanently
3. **Action economy loss**: If your readied action goes off before your normal turn next round, you lose that normal turn

**Ambiguity - Specificity of Triggers**:
- Official examples: "if she starts casting a spell" (for counterspells)
- Community debate: How specific must triggers be?
  - Can you say "when anyone enters my reach"?
  - Or must you specify "when the orc in square E5 moves into square D4"?
- **[NO-RAW]**: Rules say trigger "must be clear" but don't define granularity

**Counterspell Special Case**: Readying a counterspell specifically requires only seeing "starts casting a spell" - you don't need to specify which spell.

## 42. DELAY ACTION - PERMANENT INITIATIVE REDUCTION

**[CAP-GATE: Combat Timing]** **[SKR-DEP: Initiative Modification]** **[C-01: Timing & Order]**

### A. Original Community Question
"I delay my turn to see what the enemy does. Can I jump back to my original initiative count, or is it changed forever?"

### B. Core Uncertainty
Whether the Delay action temporarily or permanently reduces initiative count, and what happens if you never act.

### C. Formalized Problem Statement
How does the Delay action interact with initiative order, and what are the consequences of delaying past your next turn?

### D. Source Context
- D&D 3.5 SRD: [Special Initiative Actions](https://www.d20srd.org/srd/combat/specialInitiativeActions.htm)
- EN World: ["Delay action clarification"](https://www.enworld.org/threads/delay-action-clarification.258945/)
- D&D 3e SRD: [Special Initiative Actions](https://www.dandwiki.com/wiki/3e_SRD:Special_Initiative_Actions)

**Official RAW**:
- "By choosing to delay, you take no action and then act normally on whatever initiative count you decide to act"
- "When you delay, you voluntarily reduce your own initiative result for the rest of the combat"
- "**Your initiative result becomes the count on which you took the delayed action**"

**Key Mechanics**:
1. **Permanent change**: Unlike Ready (which interrupts), Delay permanently lowers your initiative
2. **You never get back the time**: Waiting to see what happens costs you action economy
3. **Cannot interrupt**: Delay does not allow interrupting other actions (unlike Ready)

**Critical Edge Case - Delay Past Next Turn**:
- "If you come to your next action and have not yet performed an action, **you don't get to take a delayed action** (though you can delay again)"
- "If you take a delayed action in the next round, before your regular turn comes up, your initiative count rises to that new point in the order of battle, and **you do not get your regular action that round**"

**Implication**: If you delay on Round 1 (initiative 20), and don't act until Round 2 (initiative 8), your new initiative is 8 permanently, AND you lose your Round 2 initiative 20 action entirely.

**Difference from Ready**:
- **Ready**: Interrupt-capable, triggers on condition, changes initiative
- **Delay**: No interrupt, act whenever you want, changes initiative, lose actions if you wait too long

## 44. IMMEDIATE ACTION TIMING - INTERRUPT RESTRICTIONS

**[CAP-GATE: Combat Timing]** **[SKR-DEP: Action Type Classification]** **[C-01: Timing & Order]**

### A. Original Community Question
"Can I use an immediate action on my own turn, and if I use one on someone else's turn, when can I use another?"

### B. Core Uncertainty
The timing restrictions on immediate actions, particularly the swift action cost and turn limitations.

### C. Formalized Problem Statement
What are the timing and frequency restrictions for immediate actions in D&D 3.5?

### D. Source Context
- D&D 3.5 Rules Compendium / Spell Compendium
- Giant in the Playground: ["When exactly can you use an immediate action??"](https://forums.giantitp.com/archive/index.php/t-252297.html)
- Web search result: Spell Compendium PDF

**Official RAW** (from Spell Compendium/Rules Compendium):
- Immediate actions function as interrupts
- "**You cannot use another immediate action or a swift action until after your next turn** if you have used an immediate action when it is not currently your turn"

**Key Restriction**: Using an immediate action on someone else's turn costs your swift action for your NEXT turn.

**Mechanics**:
1. Immediate actions are like swift actions (very fast)
2. Swift actions are like free actions (minimal time)
3. You get one swift action OR one immediate action per round
4. If you use immediate action off-turn, you lose next turn's swift action slot

**Community Clarifications**:
- "Immediate actions are 'interrupts' and they can and should interrupt whatever is going on"
- "Free actions can be taken at any time, even in the middle of another action"

**Timing Example**:
- Initiative 20 (Wizard A): Starts casting Fireball
- Initiative 15 (Wizard B): Uses immediate action to cast Shield
- Next round, Wizard B at initiative 15: **Cannot** use swift action
- Must wait until the round after to regain swift action availability

**[CONSENSUS-WEAK]**: Some tables forget the "lose next swift" rule and allow unlimited immediate actions.

## 45. COUNTERSPELL IDENTIFICATION - SPELLCRAFT CHECK TIMING

**[CAP-GATE: Counterspelling, Identification]** **[SKR-DEP: Skill Check Resolution]** **[C-01: Timing & Order, C-09: Knowledge]**

### A. Original Community Question
"To counterspell, I need to identify the spell being cast. Is the Spellcraft check automatic, or can I fail and waste my readied action?"

### B. Core Uncertainty
Whether spell identification for counterspelling is automatic, guaranteed for prepared casters, or requires a successful skill check.

### C. Formalized Problem Statement
How does the Spellcraft check for spell identification interact with readied counterspell actions?

### D. Source Context
- D&D 3.5 SRD: [Counterspells](https://www.d20srd.org/srd/magicOverview/castingSpells.htm)
- D&D 3.5 SRD: [Casting Spells](http://dmreference.com/SRD/Magic/Magic_Overview/Casting_Spells/Counterspells.htm)
- EN World: ["Dispelling & Counterspelling Compilation"](https://www.enworld.org/threads/dispelling-counterspelling-compilation-omen_of_peace.471328/)

**Official RAW**:
- "To counterspell, you must select an opponent as the target of the counterspell"
- "**If the target of your counterspell tries to cast a spell, make a Spellcraft check (DC 15 + the spell's level) as a free action**"
- "**If the check succeeds, you correctly identify the opponent's spell and can attempt to counter it**"

**Mechanics**:
1. Ready action: "I ready to counterspell if [target] casts a spell"
2. Target starts casting
3. You make Spellcraft check (DC 15 + spell level) **as a free action**
4. **If check succeeds**: You identify spell, can counter with same spell
5. **If check fails**: You don't know what spell it is, can't counter normally
6. Alternative: Use Dispel Magic to counter without identifying (doesn't require check)

**Key Limitation**: "To identify a spell, you must see or hear the spell's verbal or somatic components"

**Spell-Like Abilities Problem** (referenced in Entry #27):
- SLAs have no components
- Cannot identify them normally
- Cannot counterspell SLAs without using Dispel Magic

**Audit Risk**: Skill check creates non-deterministic counterspell resolution - you might ready a counter and fail to execute it.

**[CONSENSUS-WEAK]**: Some tables handwave the Spellcraft check for PCs with the spell prepared.
